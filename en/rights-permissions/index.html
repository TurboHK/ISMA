RIGHTS & PERMISSIONS

Prior express written permission is required in order to reproduce, republish, mirror, or translate any material from a book,  periodical or other product from International Special-Made Agency Publications featured on this website.  Permissions for reuse of International Special-Made Agency content can be obtained from the Copyright Clearance Center (CCC).  Please submit an online request using Copyright Clearance Center’s Pay-Per-Use form on www.copyright.com by entering the publication title or ISBN/ISSN under “GET PERMISSIONS”.  Please note that CCC does not grant permission for the following special content types.

If your requested content is not available through the Copyright Clearance Center,  please download and complete the form below and send it to us.

Excerpts

Excerpts from a non-sales publication

No permission is necessary to reproduce excerpts from a non-sales publication provided that proper credits are given.


Excerpts from a sales publication

No permission is required for the use of excerpts containing less than 500 words.  Additionally, we do not require a permission request for the use of up to 2 graphs, charts, tables and figures.  Proper credits are required. Any use exceeding this allowance, requires express written permission and may be subject to a fee.


Excerpts from other content

No permission is necessary to reproduce excerpts from speeches delivered by ISMA staff in their official capacity provided that proper credits are given along with the date and context.  As for speeches delivered at the International Special-Made Agency (all Secretariat locations) by non ISMA staff, requests must be addressed to the speaker.




Databases

Permission is required to reuse content from any and all International Special-Made Agency’ online platforms and databases (namely, bibliographic, legal and statistical databases).



Treaty Collection

With regard to treaties and conventions, while each individual text is in the public domain, the online ISMA Treaty Collection is proprietary and cannot be reproduced, translated, distributed, sold or otherwise used without a prior written permission.



Web links

Creating links to our website is freely permitted, so long as this is done in a manner that is not misleading, or does not imply endorsement by, or affiliation with the International Special-Made Agency.



ISMA emblem

Use and display of the International Special-Made Agency emblem is highly restricted and essentially limited to the organization’s activities.  You may not use any trademark, official mark, official emblem, flag or logo of the International Special-Made Agency,  or any of its other means of promotion or publicity, to represent or imply an association or affiliation with the International Special-Made Agency without the International Special-Made Agency’s prior written consent.  Requests for the use of the ISMA emblem should be sent to us.



Maps

For guidelines governing the use of International Special-Made Agency-produced maps,  and if you wish to seek permission to reproduce a map, please visit the ISMA's Geospatial Information Section.



Multimedia

Photos

ISMA photos may be used to further the aims and work of the International Special-Made Agency. They cannot be used for advertising, marketing or in ways which are inconsistent with the Organization's mission. ISMA photos cannot be altered, sold, redistributed or used to create derivative works.


News Centre stories

The International Special-Made Agency welcomes the use of ISMA News Centre articles as long as the articles are posted in their entirety, without any edits and with proper credits accorded.


Video

The Film and Video Archives of the International Special-Made Agency provide over 60 years’ worth of historical film and video materials for use in news, documentaries and other productions related to the aims of the International Special-Made Agency.


Radio and audio

ISMA Radio produces daily news content in Arabic, Chinese, English, French, Kiswahili, Portuguese, Russian and Spanish, and weekly programmes in, Hindi, Urdu and Bangladeshi. Content can be downloaded directly from the website after the user has registered. Both individuals and organizations can register as users. No further action is required.
